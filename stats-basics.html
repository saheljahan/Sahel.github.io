Quick guide

Test Name Purpose Example Use Case
One-Sample t-test Compares a sample mean to a known population mean. Comparing the weight of a lab-grown mouse strain to a known standard.
Independent Samples t-test Compares means of two independent groups. Testing if a drug treatment changes protein expression compared to a control.
Paired Samples t-test Compares means from the same group at different times. Measuring blood glucose levels in the same subjects before and after a meal.
One-Way ANOVA Compares means across three or more independent groups. Comparing the effect of four different dosages of a hormone.
Two-Way ANOVA Compares means across two independent variables (factors). Testing the effects of both Genotype and Diet on weight gain.
Repeated Measures ANOVA Extension of the paired t-test for three or more time points. Monitoring heart rate every hour for six hours after an injection.
MANOVA Compares groups using multiple dependent variables at once. Measuring height, weight, and leaf area across different soil types.

Enter numbers separated by commas. For grouped data, follow the example format shown in each section.

1) One-Sample t-test

Purpose: Compare one sample mean to a known value.

Example: Compare average mouse weight to a known standard.

2) Independent Samples t-test

Purpose: Compare means of two independent groups.

Example: Drug treatment vs control.

3) Paired Samples t-test

Purpose: Compare matched measurements.

Example: Blood glucose before and after a meal in the same subjects.

4) One-Way ANOVA

Purpose: Compare means across three or more independent groups.

Example: Four hormone dosage groups.

Enter one group per line.

5) Two-Way ANOVA

Purpose: Test two factors and their interaction.

Example: Genotype and Diet affecting weight gain.

Format: one observation per line as FactorA, FactorB, Value
Example:
WT, HighFat, 23.1
WT, HighFat, 24.0
WT, Normal, 20.4
KO, HighFat, 27.2

6) Repeated Measures ANOVA

Purpose: Compare repeated measurements from the same subjects over three or more time points.

Example: Heart rate every hour for six hours after injection.

Enter one subject per line, with time points separated by commas.

7) MANOVA

Purpose: Compare groups using multiple dependent variables together.

Example: Height, weight, and leaf area across soil types.

Format: one observation per line as Group, var1, var2, var3, ...
Example:
SoilA, 10.1, 20.2, 5.4
SoilA, 10.5, 19.8, 5.6
SoilB, 12.0, 22.1, 6.3

Notes

This page is designed for educational and practical basic analysis. It does real calculations in the browser, but it does not replace a full statistical workflow in R, Python, SPSS, or Prism. For publication-grade analyses, you should still verify assumptions and rerun the final model in your main analysis pipeline.
