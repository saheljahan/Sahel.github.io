Why this page?

This page gives a beginner-friendly summary of basic statistical tests often used in biology, data analysis, and research. It is meant as a quick guide to help choose the right test.

t-test

What it is: A test used to compare the means of two groups.

Best for: Checking whether one group is significantly different from another, like treatment vs control.

Two groups

Paired t-test

What it is: A version of the t-test used when the two measurements are related.

Best for: Before vs after comparisons in the same samples or subjects.

Matched samples

ANOVA

What it is: A test used to compare the means of three or more groups.

Best for: Experiments with multiple conditions, treatments, or time points.

Three or more groups

Chi-square test

What it is: A test used for categorical data to see whether observed counts differ from expected counts.

Best for: Tables of counts, proportions, or category frequencies.

Categorical data

Correlation

What it is: A measure of how strongly two variables are related.

Best for: Seeing whether two continuous variables move together, like gene expression and phenotype.

Relationship between variables

Linear Regression

What it is: A method to model the relationship between one outcome and one or more predictors.

Best for: Predicting outcomes and testing how variables affect a response.

Prediction and association

FDR correction

What it is: A way to adjust p-values when doing many tests, to reduce false discoveries.

Best for: RNA-seq, genomics, or any analysis with multiple comparisons.

Multiple testing

Non-parametric tests

What it is: Tests that do not assume normal distribution of the data.

Best for: Small sample sizes, skewed data, or when assumptions of t-tests/ANOVA are not met.

Non-normal data
